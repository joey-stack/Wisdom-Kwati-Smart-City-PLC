#### Overall Impression
The page is functional but structurally misses the mark on the brand's identity. It relies on startup tropes (glass, playful copy, icon grids) instead of restrained, high-contrast, editorial elegance. The biggest opportunity is rewriting the copy and stripping away the SaaS UI patterns in favor of bold typography and raw imagery.

#### What's Working
- **Content Structure**: The flow from Hero -> About -> Neighborhoods -> Listings -> Services is logical.
- **FAQ Component**: The accordion layout for FAQs is a proven, effective pattern.

#### Priority Issues

- **[P0] Brand Identity Violation: Glassmorphism & SaaS Tropes**
  - **Why it matters**: The `glass-panel` hero card and icon grids directly violate the "Modern Monolith" design constraints, cheapening the luxury feel.
  - **Fix**: Remove glassmorphism. Use solid, high-contrast structural blocks. Replace the services icon grid with an editorial typographic list or image-driven layout.
  - **Suggested command**: `/impeccable shape`

- **[P1] Copy Tone: Too Casual**
  - **Why it matters**: Phrases like "real estate sidekick" and "Take a peek" destroy the "luxury-heritage editorial" vibe. 
  - **Fix**: Rewrite headings to be authoritative, restrained, and structural.
  - **Suggested command**: `/impeccable clarify`

- **[P1] Cognitive Load: Service Cards**
  - **Why it matters**: Presenting 5 equal-weight service cards simultaneously in an icon grid creates visual noise and feels generic.
  - **Fix**: Use progressive disclosure or an alternating editorial layout for services to give them breathing room.
  - **Suggested command**: `/impeccable layout`
